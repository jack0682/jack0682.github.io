An **Ontology Neural Network** (ONN) is a neural model whose internal
state is not a flat vector but a typed object carrying the structure
of a target ontology: inference is a constraint-projection onto a
topology-aware manifold. The companion **ORTSF** (Ontological Real-Time
Semantic Fabric) framework turns the output of that inference into a
closed-loop controller.

The programme's organising bet was that **the topology of a
representation space is itself a control-theoretic object** — that the
same higher-order/cohomological invariants which classify learned
features would also certify the loop closed around them. That bet was
put under a formal audit, and **it did not survive in its strong form.**

<Callout tone="warning" title="Current state — a scoped No-Go">
The central higher-order thesis resolved to an **identifiability
boundary** (the "Two Ceilings"): under a complete-pairwise contract on
consistent, complete, finite scenes, measured higher-order /
cohomological structure adds **no information beyond pairwise**. It is a
*scoped* No-Go — it has named escapes (incomplete/partial pairwise
observation, non-generic regimes) — not a blanket refutation. See the
[canonical spec](/onn/canonical-spec/) and the
[research status](/onn/onn-research-status-2026/) for what stands and
what was withdrawn. Active work has moved to a successor line (ULR).
</Callout>

<Callout tone="note" title="Where this sits">
ONN is Layer 3 of the larger cohesion-first architecture — downstream
of the discovery of stable structure (Layer 2: [RelationWorld](/research/theory/))
and upstream of embodied action (Layer 4: [Delay-robust control](/research/control/)).
The programme's design target — unifying ONN with Soft Cognitive Cohesion
through a single cohomological certificate — is spelled out in
[Integrated Architecture](/notes/part-0/integrated-architecture/), where
the ONN/ORTSF side of that coupling is now marked *not established*.
</Callout>

## What survived the audit

- **A modest, non-higher-order positive.** One signal held up under the
  audit: a small direction-channel effect (a Q-weak improvement of
  roughly +0.11–0.12 AUROC). It does **not** come from higher-order
  structure — it is the honest positive result of the programme.
- **A standard delay-margin certificate.** The ORTSF control result
  reduces to a textbook delay margin ($\Delta t_{\max} = \varphi_{\mathrm{PM}}/\omega_c$)
  plus a sufficient small-gain condition — *decoupled* from the
  ontology's cohomology. See
  [the certificate](/onn/theorem-delay-robust-bound/).
- **The audit methodology.** The pre-registered contract, the
  consistent/complete/finite scene conditions, and the falsification
  discipline that turned an appealing thesis into a checkable boundary.

## Earlier manuscripts (superseded framing)

These 2025 drafts state the *original* optimistic framing. Their
headline empirical numbers (e.g. "99.75 % of predicted optimality", a
constructive-Lyapunov "60-year gap" closure) are **not reproducible from
the current research source** and are superseded by the audit above; they
are retained as manuscripts of record with a status banner on each page.

- [*ONN for Topologically Conditioned Constraint Satisfaction*](/papers/onn-constraint-satisfaction/) — the Forman–Ricci / Deep-Delta projection line.
- [*Advanced Topology-Preserving Neural Networks*](/papers/advanced-onn-ortsf-extension/) — the empirical-optimality follow-up.
- [*Constructive Lyapunov Functions via Topology-Preserving Neural Networks*](/papers/constructive-lyapunov-onn/) — the Lyapunov-from-loss claim.
- [*An Axiomatic Framework for Understanding Relations via Gauge-Invariant Cohomology*](/papers/axiomatic-cohomology-relations/) — the formal cohomology scaffolding.
