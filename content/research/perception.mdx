Most accounts of perception start from objects and ask how they
relate. This track starts one step earlier and asks: *what holds
anything together enough to become an object in the first place?*

The answer offered here is **Soft Cognitive Cohesion (SCC)**. Its
primitive is a graded field

$$
u_t : X_t \to [0, 1]
$$

over a relational support space, where $u_t(x)$ measures the degree
to which site $x$ participates in a cohesive formation. It is not a
probability, not a segmentation mask, and not a clustering assignment.
Objects are a distinguished limit of this formation space, not its
starting point.

## Formal structure in one paragraph

\mathfrak{C}^{\mathrm{soft}} = \Big( \mathbb{T},\ \{X_t\},\ \{u_t\},\ \{\mathrm{Cl}_t\},\ \{\mathbf{N}_t, \mathbf{D}_t\},\ \{\mathbf{M}_{t \to s}\} \Big)

</Theorem>

## Commitments that shape the theory

<Callout tone="note" title="Ontological priorities">
- The soft field is primitive; crisp objects are derivative.
- The four energy terms are conceptually independent and never collapsed.
- Distinction is the self-contrast of a field against its exterior, not an edge detector.
- Closure has a stabilisation tendency, not primitive idempotence.
- The theory is not fuzzy segmentation, not clustering, not tracking.
</Callout>
