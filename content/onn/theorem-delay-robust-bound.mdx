*Rewritten 2026-07-10 against `onn_ws/ONN`. The earlier version stated
$\tau_{\max} = 177\;\mu\text{s}$ "for 3M-node networks" and derived stability from
a cohomological Lyapunov function — neither of which is in the source. The old
published control numbers ($\text{dt}_{\max} = 52\text{ ms}$, phase margin
$28°$) are not regenerable and are dropped. This is the certificate that is
actually proved and enforced.*

## Statement (delay margin)

For the ORTSF closed loop over the proved plant
$G(s) = C(sI + M)^{-1}B$ with $M = L_F + \mu\,\Pi_{H^\perp}$ (poles
$-(\lambda_i + \mu)$, dominant pole $p = \lambda_2 + \mu$), the phase margin is

$$
\varphi_{\mathrm{PM}} = \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan\!\left(\frac{\omega_c}{p}\right),
$$

and the closed loop tolerates any communication delay up to

$$
\Delta t_{\max} = \frac{\varphi_{\mathrm{PM}}}{\omega_c}.
$$

This is **proved and enforced at runtime**: the `GuardedORTSFPipeline` computes
$\Delta t_{\max}$ from the (computed, not hand-built) spectrum, admits commands
while $\text{delay} < \Delta t_{\max}$, and falls back to a zero-velocity command
when $\text{delay} \geq \Delta t_{\max}$ (verified in the integration tests to
$10^{-12}$).

## Nonlinear small-gain certificate (sufficient, conservative)

If the ONN spine contraction $\rho_{\mathrm{ONN}} = \rho_{\mathrm{drift}} < 1$ and
the ORTSF gain $\gamma_{\mathrm{ortsf}}$ satisfy

$$
\rho_{\mathrm{ONN}} \cdot \gamma_{\mathrm{ortsf}} < 1,
$$

the interconnected loop is stable for **arbitrary delay** — a *delay-independent*
guarantee. It is **sufficient but not tight** (a conservative envelope), validated
by simulation: sound where it holds, non-vacuous where it fails.

## Honest scope

- **This is systems completion, not novelty.** Delay-robust control is standard;
  the margin above is a linearized-plant phase-margin result plus a conservative
  small-gain envelope. The `[GAP-NL]` nonlinear case is only partially closed.
- **The margin is *not* set by the graph spectrum $\lambda_2$.** A larger
  $\lambda_2$ only moves the plant pole; the crossover $\omega_c$ (hence the
  margin) is set independently and the controller can re-gain. The earlier
  "delay margin governed by $\lambda_2$ / by cohomological invariants" coupling
  was refuted.
- **No numeric bound is claimed as a physical result.** The certificate is
  symbolic and enforced; the only concrete figure in the source is an
  *illustrative* $\Delta t_{\max} = 0.49\text{ s}$ in an end-to-end demo.
