## Statement

**Theorem (Delay-Robust Bound for ORTSF).**
Consider the ORTSF (Ontology-Resolved Topological State Feedback) closed-loop system driven by an ONN whose total loss $\mathcal\{L\}_\text\{total\}(S, A)$ serves as a Lyapunov certificate. Let $\tau$ denote the one-way communication delay between the perception (ONN) subsystem and the controller.

There exists a critical delay

$$
\tau_\text\{max\} = 177\;\mu\text\{s\}
$$

such that for all $\tau \leq \tau_\text\{max\}$ the closed-loop system satisfies:

1. **Asymptotic stability.** The equilibrium of the delay-differential system is asymptotically stable with exponential convergence rate.
2. **Topology preservation.** The Betti numbers of the latent representation remain invariant under the delayed feedback.
3. **Input-to-State Stability (ISS).** For bounded disturbances $\lVert d(t) \rVert \leq \bar\{d\}$, the state deviation remains bounded by a class-$\mathcal\{KL\}$ function of the initial error plus a class-$\mathcal\{K\}$ function of $\bar\{d\}$.

The bound $\tau_\text\{max\} = 177\;\mu\text\{s\}$ is established for a 3M-node semantic network. {/* TODO: state how tau_max scales with network size (conjectured inverse relationship with node count) */}

1. **Lyapunov candidate.** The ONN total loss $\mathcal\{L\}_\text\{total\}(S, A)$ -- combining semantic consensus loss, topological connection loss, and contextual constraint loss -- is shown to be positive definite and radially unbounded with class-$\mathcal\{K\}_\infty$ bounds:

$$
\alpha_1(\lVert x \rVert) \leq \mathcal\{L\}_\text\{total\}(x) \leq \alpha_2(\lVert x \rVert)
$$

where $\alpha_1, \alpha_2 \in \mathcal\{K\}_\infty$ are explicitly constructed. {/* TODO: fill exact forms of alpha_1 and alpha_2 from the paper */}

\tau_\text\{max\} = \frac\{1 - \mu_\text\{diag\}(1 + \kappa_\text\{off\})\}\{\lambda_\text\{max\}(J_\text\{ONN\}) \cdot \hat\{\Gamma\}\}
