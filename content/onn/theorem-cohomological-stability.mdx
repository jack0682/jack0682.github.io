## Statement

**Theorem (Cohomological Stability Certificate).**
Let $G = (V, E, w)$ be an ontology graph equipped with a $U(1)$ gauge connection, and let $([A_\infty], \Sigma, e)$ be its gauge-invariant existence triple as defined in the [cohomology paper](/papers/axiomatic-cohomology-relations/). Let $G' = (V, E, w')$ be a perturbation of $G$ with $\lVert w - w' \rVert_\infty \leq \varepsilon$.

Then the three cohomological axes are stable in the following sense:

1. **Intrinsic cohomology.** For sufficiently small $\varepsilon$,

$$
H^k(K_\alpha;\, \mathbb\{F\}) \;\cong\; H^k(K'_\alpha;\, \mathbb\{F\}), \quad k = 0, 1, \ldots
$$

where $K_\alpha$, $K'_\alpha$ are the fruit subcomplexes of $G$ and $G'$ respectively.

2. **Relative cohomology.** The pair cohomology $H^k(K_\alpha, L_\alpha;\, \mathbb\{F\})$ -- capturing the aggregate-singularity (fruit-door) interface -- has rank given by explicit matrix formulas that are Lipschitz-continuous in $\varepsilon$:

$$
\bigl\lvert \operatorname\{rank\}\, \partial_k(G) - \operatorname\{rank\}\, \partial_k(G') \bigr\rvert \;\leq\; C_k \cdot \varepsilon
$$

for constants $C_k$ depending on the combinatorial structure of $G$. {/* TODO: fill exact form of C_k from the paper or derive from the rank formulas */}

3. **Persistence barcode stability.** The bottleneck distance between the persistence barcodes of $\Sigma(G)$ and $\Sigma(G')$ satisfies:

$$
d_B\bigl(\operatorname\{Dgm\}(\Sigma(G)),\; \operatorname\{Dgm\}(\Sigma(G'))\bigr) \;\leq\; \varepsilon
$$

by the classical stability theorem for persistent homology (Cohen-Steiner, Edelsbrunner, Harer 2007).

4. **Long exact sequence preservation.** The long exact sequence relating the three axes (Theorem 2 of the [cohomology paper](/papers/axiomatic-cohomology-relations/)) is natural with respect to the perturbation, so the connecting homomorphisms vary continuously.
