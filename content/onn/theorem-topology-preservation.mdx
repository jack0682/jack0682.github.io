*Rewritten 2026-07-10 against `onn_ws/ONN`. This page previously stated a
theorem (Betti numbers preserved, a "CSR $\to 1.0$" metric) that is **not
present in the research source**. It is corrected here to the audited result.*

## The claim as originally stated

That the ONN operator $T = T_{\mathrm{proj}} \circ T_{\mathrm{consensus}}$ drives
a "Constraint Satisfaction Rate" $\operatorname{CSR}(T^n(x_0)) \to 1.0$ and
preserves the Betti numbers of the latent embedding,
$\beta_k(\mathcal{R}(T^n(x_0))) = \beta_k(G)$.

## What the audit found

- **No `CSR` metric, and no `11.68 → 1.15` topology-loss trajectory, exists in
  the source.** Neither the metric nor those numbers appear anywhere in the code
  or theory docs; they are removed.
- **The real, defensible result is weaker: harmonic-subspace preservation.** For
  an H-shift-invariant update $g$ and a **hard** H-aligned anchor, the harmonic
  component is preserved, $\Pi_H(T\xi) = \Pi_H \xi$. This is a genuine
  proposition — but a conditional one.
- **The soft-anchor version is refuted for $\dim H > 0$:** the soft Tikhonov pull
  re-injects an H-component and breaks preservation. Only a hard, H-aligned
  anchor preserves.
- **Harmonic No-Go (locked):** even where the harmonic subspace *is* preserved,
  it carries **zero information** through the spine channels ($I = 0$; the
  consensus $\Pi_H \xi^\star$ is *specified by the anchor, not learned from
  data*). So "preservation" here is not a learned representational property.

## Takeaway

"Topology preservation" in the strong, headline sense (Betti preserved, CSR
$\to 1.0$, validated to millions of nodes) is **not established**. The honest
residue is a narrow, conditional harmonic-subspace identity whose preserved part
is anchor-specified rather than learned. This sits alongside the broader
[Two Ceilings No-Go](/onn/canonical-spec/) on the information content of
higher-order structure.
