## What ORTSF is

**ORTSF** turns the ONN's typed reasoning trace into delay-robust control
commands. Its verified content is a **standard control certificate** — a delay
margin plus a small-gain condition — *not* a cohomological one. The stability
is driven by the plant pole $p = \lambda_2 + \mu$, and is deliberately **not**
coupled to the graph spectrum $\lambda_2$ or to any cohomology class.

## Operator family

| Operator class | Symbol / mechanism | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Projection** | $T_{\text{proj}}$ | Projects the ONN latent state onto the typed fibre of the target ontology. |
| **Consensus** | $T_{\text{cons}}$ | Aggregates neighbouring fibres toward local agreement. |
| **Composite** | $T = T_{\text{proj}} \circ T_{\text{cons}}$ | The core update; a spine contraction $\rho_{\mathrm{ONN}} = \rho_{\mathrm{drift}} < 1$. |
| **Guarded pipeline** | `GuardedORTSFPipeline` | Computes the delay margin from the plant spectrum and admits commands only while $\text{delay} < \Delta t_{\max}$; else falls back to a zero-velocity command. |
| **Drift guard** | KL-divergence monitor (A3$'$) | Detects regime shifts that would invalidate the stationarity assumption. |

## The verified certificate

**Delay margin (proved, enforced):**

$$
\Delta t_{\max} = \frac{\varphi_{\mathrm{PM}}}{\omega_c}, \qquad
\varphi_{\mathrm{PM}} = \frac{\pi}{2} - \arctan\!\left(\frac{\omega_c}{p}\right),
\quad p = \lambda_2 + \mu.
$$

**Small-gain (sufficient, conservative, delay-independent):** if
$\rho_{\mathrm{ONN}}\cdot\gamma_{\mathrm{ortsf}} < 1$, the loop is stable for
arbitrary delay. Full statement and scope on the
[delay-margin certificate page](/onn/theorem-delay-robust-bound/).

## Honest scope

- **Systems completion, not novelty** — delay-robust control is standard; this
  is a linearized-plant margin plus a conservative small-gain envelope.
- **No cohomological stability bounds.** The claim that cohomological invariants
  double as Lyapunov certificates is [withdrawn](/onn/theorem-cohomological-stability/).
- **No `τ_max = 177 μs`, no `99.75%`.** These are not in the source and are removed.
- **Ontology-morphism $\to$ feedback-gain functor:** proposed, **not established**;
  left as an open direction.

## Open directions

Global (non-conditional) stability; tightening the nonlinear small-gain
`[GAP-NL]` (currently only sufficient); transfer of the margin to
transformer-backbone variants.
