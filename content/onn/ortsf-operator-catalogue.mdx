## What ORTSF Is

The **Ontology-Resolved Topological State Feedback** (ORTSF) framework is
a family of predicate-binding operators that compose typed features
produced by the Ontology Neural Network (ONN) under communication and
sensing delay, with provable stability bounds stated in cohomological
terms. Its central idea is that the topology of the learned
representation space is itself a control-theoretic object: recognising
this allows delay-robustness guarantees to be expressed as cohomological
invariants of the learned ontology, and allows controllers to exploit
precisely that structure.

## Delay-Robust Control

ORTSF guarantees bounded behaviour under communication delay up to a
maximum $\tau_{\max}$ derived from the topology of the ontology graph.
The key condition is the **delay-coupling small-gain inequality**:

$$
\mu_{\text{diag}} < \frac{1}{1 + \kappa_{\text{off}}}, \qquad
\kappa_{\text{off}} = \eta_{\text{off}}^{95} \cdot m_{\text{off}}^{95}
$$

where $\mu_{\text{diag}}$ is the diagonal contraction rate and
$\kappa_{\text{off}}$ captures the 95th-percentile off-diagonal
coupling. When this holds, the delay-amplification envelope $\hat\Gamma$
remains bounded, and the system is stable for all delays
$\tau \le \tau_{\max}$.

## Ontology Morphism to Feedback Gain Map

A structural change in the ontology graph — addition or removal of types,
edges, or predicates — constitutes an **ontology morphism**. The ORTSF
framework establishes a correspondence between such morphisms and
adjustments to the closed-loop feedback gains:

- **Cohomological invariants** of the learned ontology serve
  simultaneously as representational descriptors and as Lyapunov-type
  certificates for the control loop.
- When a morphism alters the cohomology class, the associated Lyapunov
  certificate must be recomputed, which translates directly into an
  updated gain schedule for the predicate-binding operators.

## Integration with ONN

ORTSF operators consume the ONN's **typed latent state** — a 64-dimensional
semantic fibre ($B_{16} \oplus F_{32} \oplus I_{16}$) — rather than a
flat vector embedding. This coupling is what gives the framework its
topological character:

1. ONN trains with a type-algebra-preserving loss, producing a latent
   state that carries the ontology's structure by construction.
2. ORTSF reads that typed state and applies $T = T_{\text{proj}} \circ
   T_{\text{cons}}$ to enforce consensus across the ontology graph.
3. The LOGOS solver drives the composite system to a fixed point under
   ABS\_ENERGY, REL\_ENERGY, and MAX\_ITERATIONS convergence criteria.
4. Monitoring modules (drift guard, quantile gates) run concurrently,
   triggering regime-conditional re-calibration when A3$'$ or A4$'$
   assumptions are violated.
