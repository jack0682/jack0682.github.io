## Bottom line

The ONN / ORTSF programme's central question — *is measured higher-order
relational structure a usable discriminative resource beyond pairwise?* —
resolved to a **rigorous negative / boundary result**, not a positive
framework. `onn_ws/ONN` is now archived; live work continues under **ULR**.

## What is settled

1. **The Two Ceilings (No-Go), proven.** Under a *complete-pairwise observation
   contract*, on consistent, complete, finite-$\pi_1$ scenes, every admissible
   measured higher-order readout is pairwise-measurable, exact/zero,
   label-injected, or noise — so a Bayes-optimal pairwise decoder gains **no
   information** from it. Witness: the $\operatorname{coker}\delta_1$ area datum
   is analytically reconstructible from pairwise positions to the noise floor
   (residual $0.050 \approx \sigma$). Scope: does **not** broadly refute
   higher-order/sheaf networks — each escape violates a named hypothesis
   (incompleteness, dynamics, external sensor, infinite-cd topology, bounded
   capacity).
2. **A modest, non-higher-order positive.** The *direction* of the measured
   curvature field carries signal beyond magnitude (E1b Q-weak $= +0.115/+0.120$
   AUROC, pass-fraction $1.0$; selection-corrected single-arm $\approx +0.08$–$0.10$).
3. **An ORTSF control certificate (standard, scoped).** A proved and
   runtime-enforced delay margin $\Delta t_{\max} = \varphi_{\mathrm{PM}}/\omega_c$
   plus a sufficient small-gain ($\rho + K_c < 1$) delay-independent certificate.
   "Systems completion, not novelty." See
   [the certificate page](/onn/theorem-delay-robust-bound/).
4. **Methodology finding.** A learner is neither necessary nor sufficient to judge
   an information / reconstructibility claim — decide analytically (false positive
   $E_{w_2}$, false negative $P_{\mathrm{coker}}$).

## What was refuted / did not survive audit

- The **higher-order loop advantage** (the once-headlined "loop beats edge $+0.165$")
  does **not** survive (E1b Q-strong $+0.045/+0.073$, Holm $p = 0.22$, GRAY).
- **"Topology preservation" as stated** (Betti preserved, a "CSR $\to 1.0$"
  metric): the real result is conditional *harmonic-subspace* preservation only
  (hard anchor); the soft-anchor version is refuted for $\dim H > 0$. See the
  [audit](/onn/theorem-topology-preservation/).
- A **cohomological Lyapunov certificate**: no such object exists. See the
  [audit](/onn/theorem-cohomological-stability/).
- The **original paper's 7–8 grand theorems**: 0 of 8 survive as written.
- **Every legacy number** — `CSR = 1.0`, `τ_max = 177 μs`, "3M-node", `99.75%`,
  topology-loss `11.68 → 1.15`, `c_J ≤ 0.7` — is **not present in the source**
  and has been removed.
