## What SCC is, in one breath

A mathematical theory of how **coherent formations emerge prior to
discrete objecthood**. The primitive is a soft cohesion field

$$
u_t : X_t \to [0, 1]
$$

over a relational support space. Objects are a distinguished limit of
the formation space, not its starting point. Four independent energy
terms govern closure, separation, boundary, and transport; a
diagnostic vector $\mathbf{d} = (\mathrm{Bind}, \mathrm{Sep}, \mathrm{Inside}, \mathrm{Persist})$
reads off formation state.

## Open problems (as of 2026-04-12 audit)

Three unresolved items that currently gate consolidation toward the
next canonical version:

- **OP-0001 · F-1** — Foundational assumption unexplicated in the
  theorem statements (fixed $K$ manifold). Needs promotion from
  implicit to explicit in the canonical text.
- **OP-0002 · M-1** — Measure-theoretic assumption on the transport
  kernel that is currently carried silently through K-field results.
- **OP-0003 · MO-1** — Modelling assumption about closure
  idempotence vs. stabilisation tendency; the theory deliberately
  omits primitive idempotence (A3 stabilisation only), but some
  downstream arguments implicitly assume it.

Separately, the **exp65 validation failure** (2026-04-12) has
prompted the Type A / Type B classification to be retracted and
replaced with a more careful taxonomy.

## The Research OS reboot (2026-04-12)

The project's organisational substrate was rebuilt into a **Research
OS** — an 8-layer structure designed to prevent silent assumptions
and make every claim traceable to its axioms and experiments.

- **Layer 0** — Project root (`RESEARCH_OS_MASTER_INDEX.md`, `CLAUDE.md`).
- **Layer 1** — Constitutional foundation (`00_meta/`): ontology,
  naming convention (`D-`, `S-`, `T-`, `A-`, `OP-`), status codes,
  promotion rules, five agent roles.
- **Layer 1b** — Immutable registries (`03_context_memory/`):
  concept registry (29 entries), symbol registry (46), theorem
  registry (48 total: 43 proved + 5 with caveats), assumption
  registry (35, including F-1 / M-1 / MO-1 now explicit).
- **Layers 2–7** — Canonical specs, roadmap, questions, claims,
  proofs, counterexamples, experiments, results, papers,
  discussions, archive, figures, scripts, templates.

The net effect: every statement in the theory is now tagged with its
dependency chain, and no claim can be promoted to canonical status
without passing an audit pass through the registries.
