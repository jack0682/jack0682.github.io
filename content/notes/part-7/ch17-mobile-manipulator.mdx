## 17.1 Purpose and Scope

This chapter instantiates RelationWorld theory for a practical mobile manipulator architecture:

- **Mobile base**: 4-wheel swerve drive on the plane
- **Manipulator**: 4-DOF arm
- **Sensors**: RGB/depth camera, 2D/3D LiDAR, IMU, odometry, joint states, optional force/contact

The aim is not to replace Chapters 1--16, but to provide an embodiment-level map:

46452
\text\{raw sensor\} \to \mathfrak W_t \to r_t \to g_t^\{\mathrm\{task\}\} \to \xi_t \to u_t.
$$

## 17.2 Ontological Role of the Robot

In this embodiment, the robot is specialised as:

46452
\text{mobile manipulator} = \text{stem traversal} + \text{door manipulation}.
$$

Hence, its operational priority is not arbitrary in-fruit dexterity, but reliable traversal over stem regions and intervention at door boundaries.

## 17.3 State and Action Model

### 17.3.1 Base state (swerve)

46452
x_t^b = (x_t, y_t, \theta_t), \qquad
\nu_t = (v_\{x,t\}, v_\{y,t\}, \omega_t).
$$

Discrete-time kinematics (time step $\Delta t$):

46452
\begin{aligned}
x_{t+1} &= x_t + (v_{x,t}\cos\theta_t - v_{y,t}\sin\theta_t)\Delta t, \\
y_{t+1} &= y_t + (v_{x,t}\sin\theta_t + v_{y,t}\cos\theta_t)\Delta t, \\
\theta_{t+1} &= \theta_t + \omega_t\Delta t.
\end{aligned}
$$

### 17.3.2 Arm state

46452
q_t^a = (q_\{1,t\},q_\{2,t\},q_\{3,t\},q_\{4,t\}), \qquad
\dot q_t^a = (\dot q_\{1,t\},\dot q_\{2,t\},\dot q_\{3,t\},\dot q_\{4,t\}).
$$

### 17.3.3 Combined robot state and action

46452
x_t^r = (x_t^b, q_t^a, \nu_t, \dot q_t^a).
$$

46452
u_t^\{\mathrm\{mb\}\} = (v_\{x,t\}, v_\{y,t\}, \omega_t, \dot q_\{1,t\}, \dot q_\{2,t\}, \dot q_\{3,t\}, \dot q_\{4,t\}).
$$

## 17.4 Base-Arm Coupled Kinematics

Let end-effector pose be

46452
p_t^e = f_{\mathrm{FK}}(x_t^b, q_t^a).
$$

Then velocity is

46452
\dot p_t^e = J_b(x_t^b,q_t^a)\nu_t + J_a(x_t^b,q_t^a)\dot q_t^a,
$$

or compactly

46452
\dot p_t^e = J_{\mathrm{whole}}(x_t^r)\,\nu_t^{\mathrm{mb}}.
$$

This coupling equation is the mechanical core of the end-to-end formulation.

## 17.5 Sensor to Relational Field

Sensor stream:

46452
y_t = (I_t, D_t, L_t, \mathrm\{odom\}_t, q_t^a, \dot q_t^a, \mathrm\{force\}_t, \ldots).
$$

History encoder:

46452
z_t = E_\phi(y_{0:t}).
$$

Node/edge construction:

46452
V_t = \mathcal N_\phi(z_t), \qquad
r_t(i,j) = (i,j,w_t(i,j),g_t(i,j)).
$$

Embodiment-conditioned edge score (example):

46452
\begin{aligned}
w_t(i,j) =
&\;\alpha_1\,\mathrm{proximity}_{ij}
+ \alpha_2\,\mathrm{handover}_{ij}
+ \alpha_3\,\mathrm{cofunc}_{ij} \\
&+ \alpha_4\,\mathrm{causal}_{ij}
+ \alpha_5\,\mathrm{manipulability}_{ij}.
\end{aligned}
$$

## 17.6 World Decomposition

Given thresholds $\theta \in (0,1)$ and $\tau > 0$:

46452
F \text\{ is fruit\} \iff \phi(F) = \frac\{|\partial F|_w\}\{\mathrm\{vol\}(F)\} \le \theta,
$$

46452
S_t = V_t \setminus \bigcup_k F_k,
$$

46452
\Sigma(F)=\{i\in F : b_F(i)\ge\tau\}, \quad b_F(i)=d(i)-d_F^\{\mathrm\{int\}\}(i).
$$

World summary:

46452
\mathfrak W_t = (\{F_k\}, S_t, \{\Sigma(F_k)\}).
$$

## 17.7 Role Inference and Task Generation

Role family:

46452
\mathcal R = \{\text\{stem-transporter\},\; \text\{door-operator\},\; \text\{door-stabilizer\}\}.
$$

Role selection:

46452
r_t = \arg\max_{r\in\mathcal R}\Big[
\beta_1\Delta\mathrm{Flow}(r) +
\beta_2\Delta\mathrm{BoundaryStability}(r) +
\beta_3\Delta\mathrm{ManipulationUtility}(r)
-\beta_4\mathrm{Cost}(r)
-\beta_5\mathrm{Risk}(r)
\Big].
$$

World deficit:

46452
\Delta_t = \lambda_1\mathrm\{StemCongestion\}
+\lambda_2\mathrm\{DoorOverload\}
+\lambda_3\mathrm\{BufferMismatch\}
+\lambda_4\mathrm\{HumanBurden\}
+\lambda_5\mathrm\{IdleLoss\}.
$$

Task generation:

46452
g_t^{\mathrm{task}} = \pi_{\mathrm{task}}(r_t,\mathfrak W_t,\Delta_t).
$$

## 17.8 Navigation-Manipulation Pairing

Each task is decomposed as

46452
g_t^\{\mathrm\{task\}\} \mapsto (g_t^\{\mathrm\{nav\}\}, g_t^\{\mathrm\{manip\}\}),
$$

with canonical execution phases:

46452
\text{approach} \to \text{align} \to \text{manipulate} \to \text{exit}.
$$

For the first benchmark family (door handle/tray), these phases are mandatory.

## 17.9 Motion Latent and Hybrid End-to-End Objective

Motion latent:

46452
\xi_t = \pi_\{\mathrm\{motion\}\}(y_\{0:t\},\mathfrak W_t,r_t,g_t^\{\mathrm\{task\}\}),
\qquad
\xi_t=(\xi_t^b,\xi_t^a).
$$

Coupled objective (horizon $H$):

46452
\min_{\mathbf x^b,\mathbf q^a,\mathbf u}
\sum_{\tau=t}^{t+H}
\Big(
\ell_{\mathrm{task}} +
\lambda_1\ell_{\mathrm{world}} +
\lambda_2\ell_{\mathrm{reach}} +
\lambda_3\ell_{\mathrm{safety}} +
\lambda_4\ell_{\mathrm{energy}}
\Big).
$$

where

46452
\ell_\{\mathrm\{task\}\} = \|f_\{\mathrm\{FK\}\}(x_\tau^b,q_\tau^a)-p_\tau^\star\|^2,
$$

46452
\ell_{\mathrm{world}} =
\gamma_1\,\mathrm{dist}(x_\tau^b,\mathcal C_{\mathrm{stem}})^2
+\gamma_2\,\mathrm{door\_alignment}(x_\tau^b,\Sigma)
+\gamma_3\,\mathrm{boundary\_respect},
$$

46452
\ell_\{\mathrm\{reach\}\} = \mathrm\{IK\_residual\}(x_\tau^b,q_\tau^a,p_\tau^\star)^2.
$$

## 17.12 Validation Gates

To move from specification to simulation:

1. **Dimensional consistency** of state/action/kinematics equations.
2. **Interface completeness** for world, role, task, and manipulation action.
3. **Task closure**: door-handle/tray scenario must complete all four phases.
4. **Objective traceability**: each objective term mapped to at least one theoretical primitive.

These gates define readiness for the simulation implementation phase.
