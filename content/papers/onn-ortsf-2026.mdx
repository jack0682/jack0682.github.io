## At a glance

Most perception-for-control pipelines treat representation learning and
controller synthesis as separate problems. The central claim here is
that the **topology of the representation space is itself a
control-theoretic object**: recognising this lets us state
delay-robustness guarantees in terms of cohomological invariants of the
learned ontology, and lets us build controllers — the **ORTSF**
operators — that exploit precisely that structure.

## Contributions

- **ONN architecture.** A neural model whose internal state is a typed
  object carrying the structure of a target ontology rather than a flat
  vector. Training preserves the type algebra by construction.
- **ORTSF operators.** A family of predicate-binding gates that compose
  typed features under communication and sensing delay, with provable
  stability bounds stated in cohomological terms.
- **Representational cohomology.** A correspondence between the learned
  ontology's cohomology and classical control-theoretic Lyapunov
  certificates, making the two languages interoperable.
- **Empirical validation.** Benchmarks against transformer baselines on
  topologically-conditioned constraint-satisfaction tasks, and a
  delay-robustness study on a manipulation platform.
