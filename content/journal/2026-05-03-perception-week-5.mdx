### CV-1.5.2 (Day 6, 2026-05-02): T-L1-F Hard-Bar / Active-Count Bridge

The W5 capstone result, and the first multi-formation Cat A theorem in SCC.

| T-ID | Statement (summary) | Source |
|------|---------------------|--------|
| **T-L1-F** | Under L1-J regime $(P0)$–$(P11)$: $K_{\mathrm{bar}}^{\ell_{\min}}(U(\mathbf u); G) = K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u)$ + labeled bijection $\mathcal A_{\mathrm{bar}}: A^\varepsilon \to \mathrm{Bars}_0^{\mathrm{term}}(U; G)$ via primary representative $q_j^U = \arg\max^\prec_{x \in N_j^r} U(x)$. **Cat A conditional**. | C-0721 / P-0721 |

**What T-L1-F says, in plain language.** Take a multi-formation state $\mathbf u = (u^{(1)}, \ldots, u^{(K_{\mathrm{field}})})$ on a shared mass pool — that is, $K_{\mathrm{field}}$ slots competing for a fixed total mass $M$, where some slots may have negligible mass at any given moment. The aggregate field $U(\mathbf u) = \sum_j u^{(j)}$ is a single real-valued field on the graph. Build its $H_0$ superlevel persistence diagram with terminal-death convention: for each connected component that ever appears as you sweep the level threshold from high to low, record a bar from its birth height down to either its death height or zero. Count those bars that have length $\ge \ell_{\min}$ — call this $K_{\mathrm{bar}}^{\ell_{\min}}(U; G)$. Separately, count the slots with mass exceeding a threshold $\varepsilon$ — call this $K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon(\mathbf u)$. The theorem says: under eleven explicit hypotheses, these two integers agree, and there is an explicit bijection mapping each active slot to its corresponding dominant bar.

**The bijection $\mathcal A_{\mathrm{bar}}$ itself is the load-bearing structural content.** Prior to T-L1-F, the multi-formation theory had no canonical mechanism for *labeling* aggregate-field topological features by their generating slot. The bijection $\mathcal A_{\mathrm{bar}}(j) = $ "the unique dominant terminal-death bar with birth in $N_j^r$ at the primary representative $q_j^U$" is the answer: each active slot has exactly one dominant bar, and that dominant bar is born at the slot's local maximum (with a deterministic tie-breaker $\prec$ if needed). **Labeled multi-formation persistence** — long absent from the multi-formation theory — is now canonically supported.

**The eleven hypotheses $(P0)$–$(P11)$.** P0 fixes the terminal-death $H_0$ superlevel persistence convention. P1 fixes a deterministic tie-breaking total order $\prec$ on $X$. P2 requires active mass + connected $\delta$-support. P3 (LG-1) requires disjoint active neighborhoods $N_j^r \cap N_k^r = \emptyset$. P4 (LG-2) bounds the boundary collar: $\max_{\partial N_j^r} U \le b_j - \ell_{\min} - r_{\mathrm{assoc}}$. P5 (LG-4) imposes background suppression on $U$ (not just on the inactive residual $R_{\mathrm{inact}}$, which would be weaker). P6 fixes the birth height: $b_j \ge h_{\min} \ge \ell_{\min}$. P7 (decay-to-cut, heterogeneous form) imposes per-slot tail decay $u^{(\ell)}(x) \le \psi_\ell(d_G(x, S_\ell^\delta))$ + a bridge bound. P8 (tightened H6 on $G_j^r$) bounds the second-longest local bar: $\ell_{j,2}(u^{(j)}; G_j^r) \le \ell_{\min} - 3\rho_{\mathrm{pert}}$. P9 (NE-2 perturbation control) bounds the per-slot perturbation: $\|R_j\|_{\infty, N_j^r} \le \rho_{\mathrm{pert}}/2$. P10 bounds the inactive residual: $\|R_{\mathrm{inact}}\|_\infty \le \ell_{\min} - \rho_{\mathrm{res}}$. P11 (margin ledger) requires $h_{\min} - \max_{k \neq j} B_{jk} \ge \ell_{\min} + r_{\mathrm{assoc}} + r_{\mathrm{birth}}$ — a margin between birth heights and the worst-case bridge height between distinct slots.

**Proof structure.** Lower bound $K_{\mathrm{bar}} \ge |A|$: LG-2 boundary collar + LG-3 inter-neighborhood bridge + $h_{\min} \ge \ell_{\min}$. Upper bound $K_{\mathrm{bar}} \le |A|$: (α) LG-7 coverage (derived from LG-4 + terminal-death) ensures every dominant bar's birth lies within some active neighborhood; (β) per-neighborhood at-most-one-dominant-bar via L1-H2 Lemma 1 (graph-inclusion: $\ell_{\mathrm{glob}} \le \ell_{\mathrm{loc}}$ on $G_j^r \subseteq G$) + L1-H2 Lemma 2 (contradiction-based bottleneck-stability under tightened H6). PO-1 decay-to-cut (P7) bounds the inter-neighborhood bridge height. The L1-A through L1-L 13-step working chain in `THEORY/working/MF/` records the full derivation, including a post-L1-K external audit + L1-K-REPAIR cycle (R-1 through R-4) that closed proof-hygiene gaps before canonical merge.

**Empirical anchor.** L1-I numerical study on $T^2_{20}$ found 439/1920 (≈22.9%) configurations FEASIBLE\_WITH\_BUDGET — that is, satisfying $(P0)$–$(P11)$ within budget. Best-case parameters: $\sigma_b = 0.5$, $\delta = 0.02$, $r = 0$, $\ell_{\min} = 0.10$, raw_gaussian initialization. L1-H2 stress tests (5/5 pass), L1-J PO-1 decay-to-cut diagnostic (6/6 pass). These confirm the regime is non-vacuous, though the 22.9% coverage is honest — T-L1-F is conditional, not universal.

**P7 status decision (L1-L).** P7 (decay-to-cut) is adopted as a *safe technical regime hypothesis* rather than derived from primitive SCC dynamics. L1-L (Combes-Thomas / discrete Agmon analysis) provides theorem-grade backing under strong stationarity (P7\_DERIVED\_UNDER\_STRONG\_STATIONARITY) — but P7 is not asserted for arbitrary SCC states. This means T-L1-F is conditional in two layers: $(P0)$–$(P10)$ are direct regime conditions, and $(P11)$ is a safe technical addition with partial derivation backing.

## Process notes

### Why T-L1-F is the "first multi-formation Cat A" — and why that matters

For roughly four months, the K-field architecture (the multi-formation $\Sigma^K_M$ + shared-pool $\widetilde\Sigma_M^{K_{\mathrm{field}}}$ structures) had been substantively developed in the working/ pipeline but had not produced a single canonical Cat A theorem. Multi-formation theorems prior to T-L1-F (T-Persist-K-Sep, T-Persist-K-Weak, T-Persist-K-Unified) were Cat C — conditional on regime hypotheses that themselves were structural choices rather than derived facts.

T-L1-F is structurally different. The L1-J regime hypothesis package $(P0)$–$(P11)$ is *explicit*: each condition has a specific name, a specific role in the proof, and specific empirical / theoretical backing. P0/P1 are convention choices (terminal-death; deterministic tie-breaker). P2-P5 are local-geometry conditions. P6/P11 are height/margin conditions. P7 is decay-to-cut (the only one with an asymmetric backing — derived under strong stationarity but adopted as safe technical regime). P8/P9 are perturbation-control conditions. P10 is residual suppression. The 22.9% L1-I FEASIBLE\_WITH\_BUDGET coverage on $T^2_{20}$ is honest — T-L1-F is conditional, not universal — but it is also non-vacuous.

The labeled bijection $\mathcal A_{\mathrm{bar}}: A^\varepsilon \to \mathrm{Bars}_0^{\mathrm{term}}(U; G)$ is the structural payoff. Prior to T-L1-F, the multi-formation theory could count active slots ($K_{\mathrm{act}}^\varepsilon$, integer label count) and could count aggregate-field topological features ($K_{\mathrm{bar}}^{\ell_{\min}}$, integer hard-bar count), but had no canonical mechanism for *labeling* topological features by their generating slots. T-L1-F provides that labeling, in regime conditions that can be checked.

This matters because **labeling is the prerequisite for any multi-formation dynamics**. Without a labeled correspondence between slots and aggregate-field features, statements like "slot $j$ persists across the time interval" or "slot $j$ merges with slot $k$" cannot even be made precisely. T-L1-F (static, single-time) is the foundation; L1-N (dynamics-compatible regime persistence, W7+ work) and OP-0008 (σ^A K-jump non-determinism, σ_rich Path B in W7+) are the dynamic extensions that build on top.
